About Maryna Prymachenko

Maria Prymachenko (1909-1997) was an outstanding figure in 20th-century Ukrainian culture, admired by Picasso himself. As an Honoured Artist of Ukraine and a recipient of the Shevchenko National Prize, she holds a prominent place among artists of naïve art. Her unique world view and artistic style were influenced by traditional Ukrainian ornaments, which she incorporated into her works. Born into a peasant family in the Kyiv region, Prymachenko suffered from polio during her childhood. Undeterred, she learned embroidery from her mother and later worked at the Central Experimental Workshop of the Kyiv Museum of Ukrainian Art.
